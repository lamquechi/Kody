02 · IN THE ROOM
Influences

What's nearby while I work.

A BOOK THAT RETURNS

Bluets — Maggie Nelson

"I read it once a year. It teaches me that thinking in fragments is not the absence of structure — it's a different one."

A SONG ON REPEAT

Spiegel im Spiegel — Arvo Pärt

"Eight minutes that make a room slow down. I write to it more often than I should admit."

AN OBJECT ON THE DESK

A small ceramic cup, chipped

"Bought in Hội An, used badly, broken once, glued back. Some afternoons it is the only thing keeping me here."

A WINDOW SEEN OFTEN

Third floor, facing west

"It catches rain in a specific way that nothing else on this street catches it. Most of these pieces began at this window."

AN HOUR I KEEP

3:42 PM — the hinge

"Between afternoon and evening, when the light decides what it wants to be. The most honest hour I know."

A LINE I REREAD

"Còn nhớ là còn thương."

"From my grandmother. A six-word essay on memory I have not yet outwritten."

04 · PROCESS
Notes

How a piece arrives.

Most of my pieces begin as a temperature, not a sentence. A weather I cannot quite name — light leaving the room, an unsent message, the third hour after rain. I keep a small notebook and try to write down the temperature before I lose it.

Sometimes a piece sits in that notebook for months. Sometimes it leaves before I can return to it. I have stopped feeling guilty about either.

"Not every unfinished thing is a failure. Some are rooms we were not ready to enter."

The drafts are slow. I write in English when I want distance, in Vietnamese when I cannot avoid the heat of something. Some afternoons the language chooses me before I sit down. I trust this more than I used to.

The website you are inside of now is part of the practice, not separate from it. A reading room I built so the work would have somewhere quiet to live. I update it the way one rearranges a small apartment — not often, but with care.

If you ever read something here that stays with you, I would like to know. A small mark is enough.
